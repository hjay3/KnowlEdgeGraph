AI Engineering · Agent Architecture

Project Loom · 30 Years of Progress
JVM Concurrency Evolution — Thread to Virtual Thread
The most important change to the JVM in a decade. Virtual threads don't change semantics — they change the cost structure. The code looks the same; the scalability is orders of magnitude different. This diagram shows why the thread-per-request model now scales to millions.

Erlang BEAM vs JVM · Two Radically Different Bets
BEAM Per-Process GC vs JVM Global GC — Why It Matters
The most underappreciated architectural difference in runtime design. BEAM's process isolation means garbage collection is never global — it happens independently per lightweight process. The JVM's shared heap means even the best GC must occasionally coordinate across the whole world. This single decision explains why WhatsApp ran 900M users with 50 engineers.

Organizational Architecture · Conway's Law + Inverse
Conway's Law, the Distributed Monolith Trap, and the Inverse Maneuver
The most underappreciated insight in software engineering: your organizational structure IS your architecture. The Inverse Conway Maneuver says to design the team topology you want first — the correct architecture will follow. This diagram shows what happens when you get it wrong (distributed monolith) and how to get it right.

Distributed Monolith = microservices in name only. Multiple services deployed separately but sharing a database and calling each other synchronously.

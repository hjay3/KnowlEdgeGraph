The Navier–Stokes equations govern fluid flow: ρ(∂v/∂t + v·∇v) = −∇p + μ∇²v + F. They encode how pressure (p), viscosity (μ), and velocity (v) interact. Solving them around the wing shape — a boundary value problem — reveals the pressure differential: faster flow above = lower pressure (Bernoulli) = net upward force = lift. Stall occurs when the angle of attack is so steep that flow separates from the upper surface and the pressure difference collapses.
